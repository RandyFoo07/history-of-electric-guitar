How It Came To Be

Seeing the fruits of their labour, George Beauchamp, Paul Barth and Adolph Rickenbacker formed a partnership and created the Rickenbacker Electro Stringed Instrument Company, formerly known as the Ro-Pat-In Corporation.

In 1935, they released the first full-scale electric guitar in the world, Electro-Spanish Ken Roberts, named after Ken Roberts whom was a friend of George Beauchamp.

The guitar featured a semi-hollow mahogany body, an extended neck joining the body at the 17th fret, and a stock appointed tremolo arm that was designed and patented by Doc Kauffman. Its revolutionary design enable for playability that was never achieved in electric guitars before, and would inspire popular companies such as Fender and Gibson to utilize the design for their guitars even to this current day.

The Electro-Spanish Ken Roberts was designed to be played standing up while strapped on the body, unlike its predecessor the "Frying Pan", which was played in the 'Hawaiian style' or on the lap. To this day, it is considered to be the first modern electric guitar ever produced.
